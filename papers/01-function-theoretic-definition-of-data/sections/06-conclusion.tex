\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have argued that the informal ``raw facts'' characterization of data,
while not incorrect, lacks the structural precision needed to reason about
indexing, typing, and equality---precision that a function-theoretic
definition, $D : I \to V$ together with explicit totality, functionality,
and codomain-consistency conditions, supplies directly. We positioned this
definition against three established frameworks and showed it to be
compatible with, and presupposed by, each. We then derived four corollaries
connecting specific violations of the definition's structural conditions to
four recurring classes of systems-engineering defect, providing worked
examples showing each correspondence concretely.

Future work falls into three categories: an empirical study mining a defect
corpus to estimate how large a fraction of each defect category corresponds
to a violation of one of Conditions~1--3; full formal treatment of the
streaming and unstructured-data extensions; and static or runtime
verification tooling that checks index set and value set agreement at
serialization and deserialization boundaries.
