\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The informal ``raw facts'' characterization of data is not wrong, but it
was never going to be enough on its own. It has nothing to say about
indexing, typing, or equality---precisely the three things a
function-theoretic definition, $D : I \to V$ together with explicit
totality, functionality, and codomain-consistency conditions, settles
directly. We placed that definition next to three established frameworks
and found it compatible with, and quietly presupposed by, all three. From
there we derived four corollaries, each tying a specific violation of the
definition's structural conditions to a recurring class of
systems-engineering defect, with a worked example showing the connection
concretely rather than asserting it.

What is left falls into three buckets: an empirical study that mines a
real defect corpus to see how much of each defect category actually
traces back to a violation of Conditions 1 through 3; a fuller formal
treatment of the streaming and unstructured-data cases sketched in
Section~\ref{sec:discussion}; and tooling, static or runtime, that checks
index-set and value-set agreement at the exact serialization and
deserialization boundaries where this paper's corollaries say the
trouble starts.
